Recommendations

Align Suspect Narrative: Reconcile the suspect's gender, appearance, and role across all documents. If multiple suspects, clarify their involvement and connection to the crime.
Remove or Justify Physical Evidence: Only include vehicle/EDR/infotainment evidence if directly relevant to the fraud (e.g., suspect used vehicle to flee, or vehicle is part of the scam operation). Otherwise, focus on digital/financial evidence.
Tighten Timeline and Event Sequence: Ensure all timestamps, call logs, and incident narratives align, especially regarding 911 calls, CAD logs, and victim statements.
Improve Junk Data Integration: Include subtle, relevant clues in junk data (e.g., a phone number or address that links to the main case) to enhance analytical challenge.
Standardize Personnel Data: Use consistent badge numbers and departments for recurring officers, or clearly indicate transfers/assignments if needed.
Enhance Financial Evidence: Provide transaction records that directly reflect the fraudulent activity (e.g., gift card purchases in 2025, not unrelated ATM withdrawals from 2023).
